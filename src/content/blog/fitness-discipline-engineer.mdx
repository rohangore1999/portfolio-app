## The Parallel

I've been training seriously for 4 years. About 2 years in, I noticed something odd — my engineering improved alongside my fitness. Not because I was healthier (though that helps), but because the *discipline* transferred.

## Consistency Over Intensity

In the gym, showing up 4 times a week for a year beats 7 days a week for a month then burnout. Same in engineering. Daily code, daily reading, daily iteration compounds.

> "You don't rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems." — James Clear

## Progressive Overload → Deliberate Practice

Progressive overload means adding weight incrementally so your muscles adapt. In engineering, it's deliberately working on things slightly beyond your current level — not so hard you break, not so easy you coast.

## Recovery Is Part of the Work

Rest days aren't lazy days — they're when adaptation happens. Same with engineering: stepping away from a hard problem, sleeping on it, and coming back fresh is a skill.

## What I've Applied

- **Morning training** — protects the time, starts the day with a win
- **Tracking progress** — I log workouts like I log PRs; what gets measured gets improved
- **Showing up anyway** — some sessions are bad. Ship the PR anyway.

## Conclusion

The body and mind are one system. Investing in physical discipline doesn't take time away from engineering — it funds it.
